Participant Information Sheet

Title of the Project: How do people change structures such that they become symmetric?

Project Leader: Dr. Phil. Habil. Annette Hohenberger

Research Institution: Osnabrück University

Description of Current Research

This study investigates how people use strategies of adding or removing components to change an existing structure or state.

The experiment provides a more comprehensive understanding of how Gestalt principles—psychological principles that explain how people organize and structure visual information—affect problem-solving.

What will happen if you decide to participate in this study?

If you decide to participate in this study, you will first be provided with information about the study's purpose and procedures. You will then be required to sign a consent form to confirm that you understand the study and agree to participate. During the study, you may be asked to complete certain tasks or tests, such as filling out questionnaires or undergoing measurements. You will be kept informed about the progress and outcomes of the study. All data collected will be kept confidential and used solely for research purposes. Additionally, you have the right to withdraw from the study at any time for any reason, without any loss of rights or benefits.

Will you be paid for participating in this study?

There is no payment for participating in this study.

Sharing your health information with others

Neither your doctor nor any third party will receive any information or test results from this research. Research information and test results from this study will not be placed in your medical record or used for your medical care unless specified by the project.

What is the procedure?

You will be required to sign the ‘Participant Consent Form’ to ensure that you have fully read and understood all the information provided to you, and that you have agreed to participate in the study voluntarily. The ‘Participant Consent Form’ will be held securely, and all information contained therein will be kept strictly confidential.
